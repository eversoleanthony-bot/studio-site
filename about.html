About

Anthony Eversole

Anthony Eversole

Teaching

Dr. Anthony Eversole (he/him) is a voice teacher and acting coach specializing in adult and pediatric vocal pedagogy across classical, musical theatre, and commercial styles. His teaching combines anatomical understanding of vocal mechanics with sensory feedback, developing students' capacity to self-diagnose and solve problems independently over time.

He holds a decade of higher education experience as a voice professor, with former faculty positions at Oregon State University and the Crane School of Music at SUNY Potsdam. He currently teaches privately from his studio in Salt Lake City and online via Zoom, and is accepting new students.

Performance

As a baritone, Eversole has performed opera, musical theatre, and concert work with companies across the country. His operatic credits include principal roles with the Maryland Opera Studio, Ash Lawn Opera, Castleton Festival, Utah Lyric Opera, and the University of Oklahoma. His musical theatre work spans regional and academic productions in Utah, Oregon, and beyond.

Notable roles include Don Giovanni, Falstaff, Sharpless in Madama Butterfly, Gianni Schicchi, Sweeney Todd, and Chauvelin in The Scarlet Pimpernel. In concert, he has appeared as baritone soloist with The Baltimore Symphony, Tulsa Oratorio Chorus, Corvallis Repertory Singers, and others.

Direction & Production

Eversole has directed opera and musical theatre at university, conservatory, and regional levels, with productions mounted at Oregon State University, the Crane School of Music, and the Community Performance Series. His directorial repertoire includes works by Leoncavallo, Weill, Menotti, Puccini, Donizetti, and Simon/Norman.

He is also a working voice actor with contracts in the AI and audiobook spaces, and holds a K-12 music educator license in the state of Utah.
