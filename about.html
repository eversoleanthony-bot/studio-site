About

Anthony Eversole

Voice teacher, acting coach, director, and baritone, a former university professor who left the tenure track to build a studio where serious training and genuine joy live in the same room.

Doctor of Musical Arts, University of Maryland · faculty posts at Oregon State University and the Crane School of Music, SUNY Potsdam · based in Salt Lake City, teaching worldwide via Zoom.

Teaching Philosophy

"Good singing is a practice, patient, physical, and joyful, and every voice can learn it."

The Story

I've spent my life on both sides of the footlights: as a performer with companies like the Maryland Opera Studio, Charlottesville Opera, and the Castleton Festival, and as a teacher guiding hundreds of singers from first lessons through professional careers.

My studio welcomes everyone, with a soft spot for adults who sing in community theatre, church choirs, and shower stalls, people who were told at some point that it was too late for them. The voice is trainable at every age, and the training should feel like the best hour of your week.

When I'm not teaching, I'm directing, music directing, writing about the characters I'm building, or on stage myself. The studio stays connected to the working theatre, and so do my students.
