Voice Studio

Individualized vocal training to stabilize technique, mitigate physical tension, and build a reliable, high-performance instrument.

— Evidence-Based Mechanics Train the voice utilizing modern voice science and motor learning principles, replacing vague imagery with predictable, repeatable physical coordination.
— Text-First Exploration Integrate mechanical development with text-first exploration, ensuring technical adjustments serve the communication of dramatic truth.
— Technical Freedom Develop a deeply familiar physical coordination that reduces habitual tension and lowers the mental overhead of technique, creating space for genuine risk-taking and emotional honesty.

Role & Audition Prep

Strategic artistic preparation for singers building a principal role or entering an audition — for local and regional productions, university programs, and conservatory applications.

— Tactical Actioning Dissect script and score mechanics to unlock actionable dramatic intentions, moving past vague emotional choices into grounded storytelling.
— Gesture Economy Eliminate physical fidgeting and nervous tension, developing a purposeful economy of movement and precise eye focus that commands the stage.
— Stamina & Repeatability Build a sustainable performance framework that ensures your technical coordination and artistic choices hold up under pressure — whether in an audition room or night eight of a run.

Production Creative Leadership

Professional artistic direction and musical leadership for mainstage theatrical productions, academic institutions, and professional companies — from concept development through opening night.

— Music Direction Oversee all phases of musical preparation — from early cast vocal development through full ensemble shaping and final run-throughs.
— Stage Direction Translate deep research and concept development into compelling stage pictures, collaborating with designers to elevate the storytelling far beyond basic blocking.

The Teacher

Dr. Anthony Eversole combines functional vocal mechanics with real-world stage experience.

His perspective is shaped by professional performance experience and a diverse teaching career that includes independent studio work, institutional music education (elementary through high school), and university voice faculties.

Doctor of Musical Arts, University of Maryland  •  Master of Music, University of Oklahoma  •  Bachelor of Music, Utah State University.
