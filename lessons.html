Private instruction grounded in modern voice science and realized through emotional truth. We believe technical mastery and character work cannot be separated; true artistic vulnerability is the key to mitigating vocal tension and experiencing grounded body awareness in our singing.

Discover a safe, supportive, and casual space to explore your instrument, perfect your literature, and build a free voice completely prepared for the demands of live performance. Available in person or online.

Policy & Package FAQ

Does the studio operate in person, online, or both?

The studio operates on a hybrid model. In-person sessions are held at Day Murray Music in Salt Lake City; remote lessons are available worldwide via Zoom or FaceTime. Session format is confirmed at scheduling and can vary week to week based on student location and availability.

How do bundle credits work, and when do they expire?

Bundle credits are tracked and redeemed through Cal.com at the time of each booking. To maintain consistent vocal progress, bundles have a built-in shelf life (60 days for the 4-pack, 120 days for the 8-pack). Because studio space is limited, unused credits cannot be extended or transferred past their expiration date.

What are the technical requirements for remote lessons?

Remote instruction follows Dr. Ian Howell's vocal pedagogy audio standards. All four requirements below are non-negotiable and must be in place before the first session.

  • Wired headphones. Bluetooth is not permitted — codec-introduced latency is unacceptable in a real-time vocal instruction context.
  • External USB or XLR condenser microphone. Built-in laptop and tablet microphones are not permitted under any circumstance.
  • High-speed internet via Ethernet. Wi-Fi introduces packet jitter that degrades the audio frequencies most critical to accurate vocal feedback.
  • Zoom in Original Sound for Musicians / High-Fidelity Music Mode with background noise suppression, echo cancellation, and automatic gain control fully disabled.
  • A secondary, independent device dedicated solely to accompaniment playback. See the next question for the critical hardware rationale.
Why is a separate device required for accompaniment tracks?

Playing your backing tracks from the same laptop you use for Zoom causes audio lag and cuts off your voice when you start singing. To keep our audio clean and synchronized, you'll need to play your accompaniment from a separate secondary device—like a phone or tablet. For the best sound, I highly recommend plugging that device into a wired speaker or pairing it with a Bluetooth speaker, as just using a phone's built-in speaker can be a bit iffy.

Can I alternate between in-person and online lessons?

Absolutely. The studio is built to be completely flexible. You can book an in-person session at Day Murray Music one week and switch to an optimized remote live-stream the next. All of your package credits redeem interchangeably when booking your next session.

How do we handle sheet music and digital scores?

We keep our workflow entirely digital and seamless. You will have access to a shared workspace containing a comprehensive working PDF archive of studio exercises, public domain materials, and active repertoire. To ensure strict copyright compliance, if we dive into copyrighted contemporary or specialty repertoire, you may occasionally need to purchase an authorized digital or physical copy for your personal use.

What happens if I need to reschedule a session?

Life happens, especially when navigating performance schedules and rehearsals. You can easily modify your lesson time via your Cal.com confirmation email up to 24 hours in advance without losing your credit. Because studio space is carefully allocated, cancellations or no-shows within that 24-hour window cannot be refunded or rescheduled.
